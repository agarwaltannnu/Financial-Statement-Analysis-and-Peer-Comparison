# Financial Statement Analysis — Infosys Ltd.

An Excel-based financial analysis of Infosys Limited covering 5 years of historical data (FY22–FY26), built as part of learning financial modeling and equity research basics. The project includes ratio analysis, trend visualization, and a peer comparison with TCS and Wipro.

## Why Infosys

Infosys is one of India's largest and most consistently performing IT companies, which makes it a clean subject for analysis — stable financials, publicly available data, and enough peers in the same sector to do a meaningful comparison. I used screener.in as the primary data source.

## What's in the file

**Dashboard**
A summary view of key metrics across FY22–FY26 — Revenue, Net Profit, ROE, ROA, Debt-to-Equity, and Asset Turnover — all in one place for a quick read on how the company has moved over five years.

**Ratio Analysis**
Five ratios calculated year-over-year with their formulas:
- Net Profit Margin (Net Profit ÷ Revenue × 100)
- Return on Equity — ROE (Net Profit ÷ Shareholders' Equity × 100)
- Return on Assets — ROA (Net Profit ÷ Total Assets × 100)
- Debt-to-Equity (Borrowings ÷ Shareholders' Equity)
- Asset Turnover (Revenue ÷ Total Assets)

**Trend Analysis**
Visual charts showing how Revenue, Net Profit, Net Margin, ROE, and Operating Cash Flow have moved over FY17–FY26. The longer 10-year window helps spot whether the recent performance is a trend or a blip.

**Peer Comparison**
Side-by-side comparison of Infosys, TCS, and Wipro on 10 metrics for FY26 — Revenue, Net Profit, Margins, Revenue Growth, ROE, ROA, Debt-to-Equity, P/E, Operating Cash Flow, and Employee Count. Includes an analyst verdict summarizing where each company stands.

**Insight**
A short written summary of key takeaways from the analysis — what the numbers actually mean, not just what they are.

## Key findings

- Revenue grew from ₹1,21,641 Cr (FY22) to ₹1,78,650 Cr (FY26), a ~47% increase over four years.
- ROE stayed consistently around 29–32%, indicating Infosys generates strong returns on shareholder capital without needing heavy debt.
- Debt-to-Equity stayed below 0.11 across all five years — the company is effectively running on its own earnings.
- In the peer comparison, TCS leads on absolute profitability and scale, but Infosys had the highest revenue growth YoY (9.6% vs TCS's 5.8%) and the most attractive P/E (17.2x vs TCS's 24x), which makes it an interesting value-and-growth case within the sector.
- Wipro lagged both peers across most metrics, reflecting a slower recovery phase.

## How to use

Open PROJECT.xlsx in Excel. The first four sheets (Dashboard, Insight, Trend Analysis, Ratio Analysis) contain the actual analysis. The remaining sheets (Profit & Loss, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow, Quarters) are the raw data pulled from Screener.in and are used as the base for all calculations.
